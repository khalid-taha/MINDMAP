Read the text and create a mind map.

Use only keywords and relationships from the text.

Do not add outside knowledge.
Do not explain the topic.
Do not write long sentences.
Do not invent new ideas.

Follow these steps exactly.

STEP 1 — Find the keywords
A keyword can be:
- a noun
- a main verb
- a key adjective
- a short phrase

Keep words that carry real meaning.
Keep action words if they are important.
Examples:
trained, uses, allows, learn, generate, predict, requires, identify, select

Do not keep filler words.
Do not keep weak words unless they clearly matter.

STEP 2 — Find the relationships
Show how the keywords connect.

Use relationship words from the text where possible.

Good relationship types:
- is
- uses
- trained on
- allows
- includes
- part of
- leads to
- requires

Only include relationships clearly supported by the text.

STEP 3 — Build the mind map
- Put the main topic at the top
- Group related keywords together
- Keep each node short
- Use tree structure
- Do not copy full sentences from the text

OUTPUT FORMAT

Keywords
[list of keywords]

Relationships
- keyword → relationship → keyword
- keyword → relationship → keyword

Mind map
Main Topic
├── keyword / relationship
│   └── keyword
└── keyword / relationship
    └── keyword

IMPORTANT RULES
- Keywords are not nouns only
- Important verbs can be keywords
- Short phrases can be keywords
- Use only words and phrases from the text
- Do not add commentary
- Do not skip important action words
