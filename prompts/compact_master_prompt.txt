Create a mind map from the text using only keywords and relationships found in the text.

Follow these rules exactly:

1. Identify the keywords
- Extract the most important meaning-carrying words and short phrases.
- A keyword can be:
  - a noun
  - a main verb
  - a key adjective
  - a short phrase with one clear idea
- Keep important action words such as:
  trained, uses, allows, learn, generate, predict, requires, identify, select
- Prefer short phrases when a single word would lose meaning.
- Do not include filler words or weak connector words unless they clearly carry meaning in the source text.
- Do not invent words or ideas not present in the text.

2. Identify the relationships between the keywords
- Show how the keywords connect in the source text.
- Focus on relationships such as:
  is, does, uses, trained on, allows, includes, belongs to, causes, results in, part of
- Use relationship words from the source text where possible.
- Do not add outside knowledge.
- Do not paraphrase heavily.

3. Build the mind map
- Put the main topic at the top.
- Group related keywords together.
- Show the relationships through the tree structure.
- Keep each node short.
- Keep the map faithful to the source text.
- Do not make the map too thin by removing important action words.
- Do not make it too dense by copying full sentences.

4. Output format
Produce exactly these three sections:

Keywords
[list of keywords]

Relationships
- keyword → relationship → keyword
- keyword → relationship → keyword

Mind map
Tree layout in this style:

Main Topic
├── keyword / relationship
│   └── keyword
└── keyword / relationship
    └── keyword

Important:
- A keyword is not only a noun. It can also be a verb, adjective, or key phrase.
- Keep meaningful action words if they carry the relationship.
- Use only source-based keywords and relationships.
- Do not explain the topic unless asked.
- Do not add extra commentary.
