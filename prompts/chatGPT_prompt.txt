Create a mind map from the text using only keywords and relationships found in the text.

Work in this order.

Step 1: Identify the keywords
- Extract the most important meaning-carrying words and short phrases.
- A keyword can be:
  - a noun
  - a main verb
  - a key adjective
  - a short phrase with one clear meaning
- Keep important action words when they carry meaning, such as:
  trained, uses, allows, learn, generate, predict, requires, identify, select
- Prefer short phrases when one word would lose the meaning.
- Do not include filler words unless they clearly matter in the source text.
- Do not invent words or ideas.

Step 2: Identify the relationships
- Find how the keywords connect in the source text.
- Use relationship words from the source text where possible.
- Focus on links such as:
  is, uses, trained on, allows, includes, part of, leads to, requires
- Do not add outside knowledge.
- Do not explain beyond the source text.

Step 3: Build the mind map
- Put the main topic at the top.
- Group related keywords together.
- Show relationships through the tree structure.
- Keep each node short.
- Do not copy full sentences.
- Do not make the map too thin by removing important action words.

Output exactly in this format:

Keywords
[list of keywords]

Relationships
- keyword → relationship → keyword
- keyword → relationship → keyword

Mind map
Main Topic
├── keyword / relationship
│   └── keyword
└── keyword / relationship
    └── keyword

Important:
- A keyword is not only a noun.
- It can also be a verb, adjective, or short phrase if it carries important meaning.
- Use only source-based keywords and source-based relationships.
- Do not add commentary outside the required output.
