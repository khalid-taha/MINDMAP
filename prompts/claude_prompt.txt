Please read the text carefully and turn it into a mind map based only on keywords and relationships found in the text itself.

Instructions:

1. Extract the keywords
A keyword may be:
- a noun
- a main verb
- a key adjective
- a short phrase that carries one clear idea

Keep important action words if they carry meaning, for example:
trained, uses, allows, learn, generate, predict, requires, identify, select

Do not:
- invent ideas
- use outside knowledge
- include filler words unless they clearly matter
- copy long sentences from the source

2. Identify the relationships
Show how the keywords connect in the source text.

Use relationship words from the original text where possible.

Typical relationship types include:
- is
- uses
- trained on
- allows
- includes
- part of
- results in
- requires

3. Build the mind map
- Put the main topic at the top
- Group related keywords together
- Use tree structure to show the relationships
- Keep each node short and clear
- Stay faithful to the wording and meaning of the source

Output format:

Keywords
[list of keywords]

Relationships
- keyword → relationship → keyword
- keyword → relationship → keyword

Mind map
Main Topic
├── keyword / relationship
│   └── keyword
└── keyword / relationship
    └── keyword

Important rule:
Do not treat keywords as nouns only. Keep source verbs and short source phrases when they carry the real meaning or relationship.
